SYNC PRIMITIVES CHEATSHEET

MUTEX

var mu sync.Mutex

mu.Lock()
mu.Unlock()

Purpose:

* Protect shared state.

---

WHY NEEDED?

counter++

is:

Read
Modify
Write

Not atomic.

---

COMMON BUG

mu.Lock()

return

Forgot Unlock()

Result:
Deadlock

Use:

defer mu.Unlock()

---

RWMUTEX

var mu sync.RWMutex

Reader:
mu.RLock()
mu.RUnlock()

Writer:
mu.Lock()
mu.Unlock()

---

WHEN TO USE RWMUTEX?

When:

Reads >> Writes

Examples:

* Cache
* Config
* Lookup tables

---

ATOMIC

atomic.AddInt64()

atomic.LoadInt64()

atomic.StoreInt64()

---

ATOMIC VS MUTEX

Atomic:

* Faster
* Limited operations

Mutex:

* Flexible
* Complex state

---

SYNC.ONCE

once.Do(func() {})

Guarantees execution exactly once.

---

WAITGROUP

wg.Add()

wg.Done()

wg.Wait()

Used for waiting.

Not for protecting data.

Very common interview trap.

---

MUTEX VS CHANNEL

Mutex:

* Shared state

Channel:

* Communication
